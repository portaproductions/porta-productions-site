For Our Agents · Shoot Prep

Prepare your listing for the shoot.

A camera sees everything the eye glides past. Ten to fifteen minutes of prep per room is the single biggest thing that separates a good gallery from a stunning one — and it keeps us on schedule so your content is delivered fast. Here's exactly how to get the home camera-ready.

Lights on
Every light, every room — it warms the whole home.
Declutter
Clear surfaces read as space. Space sells.
Cars away
Empty driveway & curb for clean exteriors & drone.
Start Here

The whole-home essentials.

Do these everywhere before we arrive — they make the biggest difference in every single shot.

Light & Air

Light it up

  • Turn on every light in the house and replace any dead or mismatched bulbs.
  • Open all blinds, shades, and curtains for natural light.
  • Turn off all ceiling fans (they blur in photos) and switch off every TV.
  • Set the thermostat to a comfortable temperature for the crew.
Tidy & Clean

Clear the clutter

  • Clear countertops, tables, and nightstands down to one or two styled items.
  • Put away personal items: family photos, mail, keys, chargers, remotes.
  • Hide cords and wires wherever possible.
  • Give the home a fresh clean — vacuum, mop, dust, and wipe mirrors and glass.
Pets & Cars

Pets, cars & bins

  • Secure pets — crate them or keep them off-site during the shoot.
  • Hide pet bowls, beds, toys, and litter boxes.
  • Move all cars out of the driveway and away from the front of the house.
  • Roll trash and recycling bins out of view.
Final Touch

The little things

  • Empty sinks; no dishes, sponges, or drying racks on display.
  • Straighten rugs and vacuum so they sit clean and flat.
  • Fresh flowers or a bowl of fruit add a warm, lived-in luxury touch.
  • Walk each room from the doorway — that's the camera's view.
Room by Room

The finishing details.

A quick pass through each space the morning of the shoot.

01

Kitchen

  • Clear counters — hide the toaster, blender, coffee maker, and knife block.
  • Remove magnets, papers, and sponges; stow dish soap and towels.
  • Shine the sink and stainless steel; no dishes in sight.
  • Leave one styled item — a bowl of fruit or a single cutting board.
02

Living & Family Rooms

  • Fluff and karate-chop the pillows; fold throws neatly.
  • Clear the coffee table to a styled minimum (book, tray, candle).
  • Remove remotes, chargers, and gaming gear.
  • Straighten furniture square to the room.
03

Bedrooms

  • Make every bed hotel-style; straighten and smooth the linens.
  • Clear nightstands and dressers down to a lamp and one accent.
  • Pick up the floor and remove items from under the bed.
  • Stow laundry baskets and hampers out of sight.
04

Bathrooms

  • Clear counters — hide toothbrushes, toiletries, and soap.
  • Hang fresh, matching, neatly folded towels.
  • Close all toilet lids; remove scales and trash cans.
  • Squeegee glass, clean mirrors, and straighten bath mats.
Curb Appeal

Outside matters most.

The exterior is the first frame buyers see — and the star of every drone and video shot.

Grounds

Yard & landscaping

  • Mow, edge, and pull weeds; tidy beds and trim hedges.
  • Sweep walkways, the porch, and the patio; clean the front entry.
  • Coil and hide hoses; put away garden tools and kids' toys.
  • Straighten the doormat and keep seasonal decor minimal.
Cars & Bins

Clear the frame

  • Move every vehicle out of the driveway and off the street in front.
  • Ask neighbors, if you can, to do the same for exterior and aerial shots.
  • Hide trash and recycling bins in the garage or out of view.
Outdoor Living

Patio & pool

  • Arrange outdoor furniture and add cushions; open umbrellas.
  • Skim and run the pool so the water is clear; remove toys and the cleaner.
  • Uncover the grill if it's a feature; otherwise tuck it away.
After Dark

For twilight shoots

  • Turn on ALL interior and exterior lights, including landscape lighting.
  • Have water features and the pool running and lit.
  • We'll need access about 30 minutes before sunset — cars gone, home ready.
Day of the Shoot

A few logistics so it runs smooth.

  • Confirm access ahead of time — lockbox code, or who's meeting us on site.
  • The home is photographed as we find it on arrival, so finish prep before our start time.
  • If you can, let the home be vacant during the shoot so we can work quickly and quietly.
  • Flag anything you'd like us to feature — or any area to skip.
  • Early prep means a faster shoot and faster delivery (most galleries land in 24–72 hours).
